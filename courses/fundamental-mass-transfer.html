Fundamentals of Mass Transfer

Fundamentals of convection and diffusive mass transfer; mass transfer between phases; Separation based approaches and approaches equilibrium gradually transfer rate; stages in the design process of diffusional separation: binary and multicomponent distillation, gas absorption and liquid-liquid extraction; Stages in the design of equipment foundation contactor vapor / gas-liquid: Column Plates (tray column) and the column of bodies (packed column).

References:

  1. Seader, J. D., Henley, E. J. & Roper, D. K. (2016). Separation Process Principles: with Applications Using Process Simulators (4th ed.). John Wiley & Sons, Inc.
  2. Geankoplis, C. J., Hersel, A. A., & Lepek, D. H. (2018). Transport Processes and Separation Process Principles (5th ed.). Prentice Hall.
  3. McCabe, W. L., Harriott, P., & Smith, J. C. (2004). Unit Operations of Chemical Engineering (7th ed.). McGraw-Hill.

Mass Transfer and Diffusion

This introductory session will discuss materials about:

  1. Steady-State Molecular Diffusion
  2. Diffusion Coefficient
  3. Steady-State and Unsteady-State Mass Transfer Through Stationary Media
  4. Mass Transfer in Laminar and Turbulent Flow
  5. Models for Mass Transfer in Fluids with a Fluid-Fluid Interface
  6. Two-Film theory and Overall Mass Transfer Coefficient

Single Equilibrium Stages and Flash Calculations

Flash calculations is the fundamentals to understand component separation by boiling point difference. The materials will talk about:

  1. Gibbs' Phase Rule and Degree of Freedom
  2. Binary Vapor-Liquid Systems at Equilibrium
  3. Equilibrium Two-Phase Flash Calculations
  4. Ternary Liquid-Liquid Systems at Equilibrium

Multistage Cascades

This session discuss materials in multistage separation, including:

  1. Cascade Configurations
  2. Liquid-Liquid Extraction Cascades
  3. Distillation Cascades
  4. Membrane Cascades
  5. Hybrid Systems
  6. Degree of Freedom and Specifications for Cascades

Batch Distillation

Batch distillation is the simplest form of distillation. This session covers materials in:

  1. Differential Distillation
  2. Binary Batch Rectification
  3. Batch Stripping and Complex Batch Distillation
  4. Stage-by-Stage Methods for Batch Rectification

Binary Mixtures Distillation: McCabe-Thiele Method

McCabe-Thiele method is used to determine the operating condition of a binary distillation. This session discuss materials:

  1. Equipment for Vapor-Liquid Separations and General Design Considerations
  2. Rectifying and Stripping-Section Operating Line
  3. Feed-stage Considerations (q-Line)
  4. Number of Equilibrium Stages and Feed-stage Location
  5. Limiting Conditions
  6. Extensions of the McCabe-Thiele Method (Column Operating Pressure, Condenser, Reboiler, Feed Preheat, and Optimal Reflux Ratio)
  7. Estimation of Tray Efficiency for Distillation
  8. Packed Distillation Columns

Multicomponent Distillation: Fenske-Underwood-Gilliland Method

FUG Method, or commonly known as shortcut distillation, is a way to obtain the distillation tower initial sizing. This session discuss:

  1. Key Components Selection
  2. Operating Pressure
  3. Fenske Method: Minimum Equilibrium Stages
  4. Underwood Method: Minimum Reflux
  5. Gilliland Method: Actual Reflux Ratio and Equilibrium Stages
  6. Feed-Stage Location

Absorption and Stripping

This sessions covers materials in separation by solubility, which includes materials in:

  1. Graphical Method for Trayed Towers
  2. Kremser Group Method for Multicomponent Absorption and Stripping
  3. Stages Efficiency
  4. Rate-Based Method for Packed Columns
  5. Reactive Absorption Process

Liquid-Liquid Extraction

This session covers materials in:

  1. Equipment for Solvent Extraction
  2. General Design Considerations
  3. Hunter-Nash Graphical Equilibrium-Stage Method

Humidification Operations

This session discuss humidification which covers materials in:

  1. Humidity and Humidity Chart (Psychrometric Chart)
  2. Adiabatic Saturation Temperature
  3. Wet-Bulb Temperature
  4. Theory and Calculation for Cooling Towers

Drying of Solids

This session discuss drying, which covers:

  1. Equilibrium Moisture Content of Materials
  2. Rate of Drying
  3. Calculation Method for a Constant-Rate Drying Period
  4. Calculation Method for the Falling-Rate Drying Period
  5. Combined Convection, Radiation, and Conduction Heat Transfer in the Constant-Rate Period
  6. Equipment for Drying
  7. Equations for Various Types of Dryers

Adsorption Operations

Adsorption is a separation method which uses solids as adsorption medium. This session will discuss:

  1. Adsorbents
  2. Pure-Gas Adsorption
  3. Gas Mixtures and Extended Isotherm
  4. Liquid Adsorption
  5. Kinetic and Transport Rate
  6. Adsorption Equipment

Membrane Separations

Membrane separation becoming popular nowadays, as it is one of the most efficient separation method. The materials covered including:

  1. Membrane Materials
  2. Membrane Modules
  3. Mass Tranfer in Membranes
  4. Reverse Osmosis
