Fluid & Particles Mechanics

This course deals with fluid and particle properties corresponding to chemical engineering. Topics covered in this course include: Description of fluid and its type: Newtonian and non-Newtonian fluids; Fluid static and its application: hydrostatic pressure and pressure difference; Application of force and pressure, force on wall, and buoyancy force; Fluid flow in pipe: Reynolds number, flow regime; Energy balance for flowing fluid; Friction loss and its types; Compressible flow; Mixing and Stirring; Fluid measurement and its devices; Fluid transportation: pump, pumpâ€™s power, characteristic curve, Net positive suction head; Two-phase gas liquid flow; Solid particle system: drag force, terminal velocity; Mechanical separation: gravitation settling chamber; Centrifugation, cyclone, and filtration.

References:

  1. McCabe, W. L., Harriott, P., & Smith, J. C. (2004). Unit Operations of Chemical Engineering (7th ed.). McGraw-Hill.
  2. Munson. B. R., Young, D. F., Okisshi, T. H., & Huebsch, W. W. (2009). Fundamental of Fluid Mechanics (6th ed.). John Wiley & Sons, Inc.
  3. Nevers, N. d. (1991). Fluid Mechanics for Chemical Engineers (2nd ed.). McGraw-Hill, Inc.
  4. Richardson, J. F., Harker, J. H., & Backhurst, J. R. (2002). Coulson and Richardson's Chemical Engineering: Particle Technology and Separation Process (5th ed., Vol. 2). Butterworth-Heinemann.
  5. White, F. M. (2011). Fluid Mechanics (7th. ed). McGraw-Hill.

  7. Geankoplis, C. J. (1993). Transport Processes and Unit Operations (3rd ed.). Prentice-Hall International, Inc.
  8. Wilkes, J. O. (2018). Fluid Mechanics for Chemical Engineers with Microfluids, CFD, and COMSOL Multiphysics 5 (3rd ed.). Prentice Hall.

Properties of Fluid

As an introduction to fluid mechanics, studying properties of fluids is very critical. This session includes materials in fluid about:

  1. Density, Specific Weight, Specific Gravity, and Specific Volume
  2. Viscosity, Kinematic Viscosity, Vapor Pressure, and Surface Tension
  3. Compressibility of Fluids

Basic Equation of Fluid Flow

The materials discussed in this session covers fundamentals pricniples and equations in fluid flow, including:

  1. Mass Balances in a Flowing Fluid
  2. Equation of Motion - Differential Momentum Balances
  3. Macroscopic Momentum Balances
  4. Mechanical Energy Balances
  5. Bernoulli's Equation and Its Application

Fluid Statics

This session covers materials in static fluid and principles about static fluid, which includes:

  1. Basic Equation of Fluid Statics
  2. Pressure Variation in a Static Fluid for Incompressible and Compressible Fluid (Hydrostatic Force)
  3. Measurement of Pressure
  4. Manometry
  5. Hydrostatic Force on a Plane Surface
  6. Buoyancy Force (Archimedes' Principle)

Flow of Incompressible Fluid in Pipes

Incompressible fluid is a fluid that very familiar with our daily life, for example is water. The materials discussed includes:

  1. Macroscopic Mechanical Energy Balance
  2. Friction Loss in the Straight Pipes
  3. Friction Factor Equations and Moody Chart
  4. Flow in Non-Circular Pipes
  5. Friction Loss from Sudden Contraction, Sudden Expansion, Fittings, and Valves
  6. Application of Fluid Flow in Single Pipes and Multiple Pipe Systems
  7. Pipe Flowrate Measurement (Nozzle Meter, Orifice Meter, and Venturimeter)

Pumps

Pump is an equipment that makes incompressible fluid flows. This session discuss materials:

  1. Pump Performance Characteristics (Head, Power, Efficiency, Brake Horsepower)
  2. Pump Performance Curves
  3. Cavitation and Net Positive Suction Head (NPSH)
  4. Positive Displacement Pumps & Centrifugal Pumps
  5. Pump Selection
  6. Pump Scaling Law
  7. Fans

Flow of Compressible Fluid

Compressible fluid is a fluid that usually comes in for of gas or vapour. This session discuss:

  1. Energy Balances
  2. Mechanical Energy Balances
  3. Velocity of Sound
  4. Process of Compressible Flow
  5. Isentropic Flow Through Nozzles
  6. Friction in Compressible Flow (Adiabatic and Isothermal)

Blowers and Compressors

Blowers and compressors are the equipments to transport compressible fluids. This sessions includes materials in:

  1. Types of Blowers and Compressors
  2. Adiabatic Compression
  3. Isothermal Compression
  4. Polytropic Compression
  5. Compressor Efficiency and Power
  6. Compressor Selection
  7. Surge and Choke in Compressor
  8. Compression Ratio and Stages Required

Agitation and Mixing of Liquids

This session discus how fluid are mixed in a vessel or container, including:

  1. Agitated Vessels
  2. Classes of Impeller
  3. Flow Pattern in an Agitated Vessel
  4. Power Consumption

Rheological Properties of Fluids

One of the most important analysis in fluid mechanics are rheological analysis. This session discuss:

  1. Newtonian and non-newtonian Fluids
  2. Rheological Characteristics of Fluids
  3. Reynolds Number and Friction Factor for Non-Newtonian Fluids

Boundary Layer

This session is introduction to boundary layer. The materials in this session includes:

  1. Viscous Core and Inviscid Core
  2. Boundary Layer at Laminar and Turbulent Flow
  3. Reynolds Number for Flow Along a Flat Plate and Boundary Layer Thickness Calculation
  4. von Karman's Analysis on a Flat Plate (Drag Force, Momentum Thickness, Momentum Integral, Boundary Layer Thickness)
  5. Displacement Thickness

Motion of Particles in a Fluid

Motion of particle in fluid can effect the properties of flowing fluid. This session will discuss:

  1. Pressure and Velocity Distribution on a Spherical Surface
  2. Drag Force and Drag Coefficient Based on Flow Regime
  3. Force Balances at Terminal Velocity
  4. Terminal Velocity Based on Flow Regime
  5. Particle Separation using a Centrifuge

Fixed Beds and Fluidized Beds

Beds are commonly found in process industries, and more commonly used for catalysts. This session discuss:

  1. Random & Structured Packing
  2. Darcy's Law of Permeability
  3. Pressure Gradient for Fixed Beds
  4. Pressure Gradient for Fluidized Beds
  5. Minimum Fluidization
  6. Particulate Fluidization (Liquid-Solid System)
