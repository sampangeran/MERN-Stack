Chemical Reaction Engineering

This course provides chemical reaction engineering materials, including: Reaction engineering topology, chemical thermodynamics, batch reactor, homogeneous reaction kinetics, heterogeneous reaction kinetics, Evaluation and design of continuous ideal reactor, plug flow reactor, ideal tank reactor, introduction to non-ideal reactor.

References:

  1. Fogler, H. C. (2016). Elements of Chemical Reaction Engineering (5th ed.). Pearson Education.
  2. Levenspiel, O. (1999). Chemical Reaction Engineering (3rd ed.). John Wiley & Sons, Inc.

Mole Balances and Rate Law

This session is an introduction to reaction kinetics, including materials about:

  1. General Mole Balance Equation
  2. Batch Reactors and Continous-Flow Reactors
  3. Industrial Reactors
  4. Reaction Order and Rate Law
  5. Reaction Rate Constant
  6. Stoichiometry of Batch Systems and Flow Systems

Collection and Analysis of Rate Data

This session discuss about the analysis of reaction kinetics. The materials discussed includes:

  1. Algorithm for Data Analysis
  2. Batch Reactor Data: Differential Method of Analysis, Integral Method, and Nonlinear Regression
  3. Initial Rates Method
  4. Half-Lives Method
  5. Differential Reactors

Reaction Mechanism, Pathways, Bioreactions, and Bioreactors

This session is an introduction to biological and enzymatic reaction. The materials discussed includes:

  1. Active Intermediates and Nonelementary Rate Laws
  2. Enzymatic Reaction Fundamentals
  3. Inhibition of Enzyme Reactions
  4. Bioreactors

Catalysis and Catalytic Reactors

This session discuss materials in catalysts and the reactors that uses catalysts, including:

  1. Catalysts
  2. Catalytic Reaction Steps
  3. Synthesizing a Rate Law, Mechanism, and Rate-Limiting Step
  4. Heterogeneous Data Analysis for Reactor Design
  5. Catalysts Deactivation

External Diffusion Effects on Heterogeneous Reactions

This session introduces heterogenous reaction with the effect of external diffusion in the surface of catalyst. This session discuss materials:

  1. Fundamentals of Diffusion
  2. Binary Diffusion
  3. External Resistance to Mass Transfer
  4. Parameter Sensitivity
  5. The Shrinking Core Model

Diffusion and Reaction

This session discuss the effect of diffusion to the reaction kinetics, which includes:

  1. Diffusion and Reaction in Spherical Catalyst Pellets
  2. Internal Effectiveness Factor
  3. Falsified Kinetics
  4. Overall Effectiveness Factor
  5. Estimation of Diffusion and Reaction Limited Regimes
  6. Mass Transfer and Reaction in a Packed Bed
  7. Multiphase Reactors and Fluidized Bed Reactors

Conversion and Reactor Sizing

This session is an introduction to reactor sizing based on the reaction kinetics, which includes materials in:

  1. Definition of Conversion
  2. Design Equations for Batch Reactiors
  3. Design Equations for Flow Reactiors
  4. Reactors in Series
  5. Definition of Space Time and Space Velocity

Steady-State Isothermal Reactor Design

Steady-state isothremal reactor design session includes materials in:

  1. Design Structure for Isothermal Reactors
  2. Design of Batch Reactors
  3. Design of CSTRs
  4. Design of Tubular Reactors
  5. Pressure Drop in Reactors
  6. Mole Balances of Reactors for Liquid Phase and Gas Phase Reactions
  7. Microreactors

Multiple Reactions

Multiple reactions can be found in almost every reaction kinetics, which produces side products. This session discuss:

  1. Parallel Reactions
  2. Maximizing the Desired Product in Series Reactions
  3. Algorithm for Solution of Complex Reactions
  4. Multiple Reactions in a PFR or PBR
  5. Multiple Reactions in a CSTR
  6. Membrane Reactors to Improve Selectivity in Multiple Reactions
  7. Complex Reactions of Ammonia Oxidation

Steady-State Nonisothermal Reactor Design

The materials in steady-state non-isothermal reactor includes:

  1. Energy Balances
  2. Adiabatic Operation
  3. Steady-state Tubular Reactor with Heat Exchange
  4. Equilibrium Conversion
  5. CSTR with Heat Effects
  6. Multiple Steady States
  7. Nonisothermal Multiple Chemical Reactions
  8. Radial and Axial Variations in a Tubular Reactor

Distribution of Residence Times for Chemical Reactors

As not every reactor is ideal, the approach to non-ideal reactors is by calculating its residence time distribution. This session will discuss:

  1. Residence Time Distribution (RTD) Function
  2. Measurement and Characteristics of the RTD
  3. RTD in Ideal Reactors
  4. Reactor Modeling Using the RTD
  5. Zero-Parameter Models
  6. RTD and Multiple Reactions

Models for Nonideal Reactors

Non-ideal reactors can be approached to be ideal in some conditions. This session will discuss:

  1. One-Parameter and Two-Parameter Models
  2. Tanks-in-Series (T-I-S) Model
  3. Dispersion Model
  4. Flow, Reaction, and Dispersion
  5. Tanks-in-Series Model vs Dispersion Model
