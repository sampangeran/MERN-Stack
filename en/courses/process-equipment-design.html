Process Equipment Design

This course introduction to basic design & working principles of various commonly used process equipment, engineering standards in designing equipment, equipment & piping system layout concept in process plants. This includes Fundamental aspects of process equipment design; overview of basic mechanical design of process equipment for materials handling, mixing, heating, cooling, chemical conversion, & materials separation; overview of relevant engineering standards such as ASTM, API, etc.; fabrication, inspection & testing of equipment; understanding technical drawing & specification sheets; fundamental concepts in equipment & piping system layout; case studies in the design of plant equipment layout using plant design software.

References:

  1. Couper, J. R., Penney, W. R., Fair, J. R., & Walas, S. M. (2012). Chemical Process Equipment: Selection and Design (3rd ed.). Butterworth-Heinemann.
  2. Towler, G. & Sinnott, R. (2013). Chemical Engineering Design: Principles, Practice, and Economics of Plant and Process Design (2nd ed.). Butterworth-Heinemann.
  3. Green, D. W. & Perry, R.H. (2008). Perryâ€™s Chemical Engineering Handbook (8th ed.). McGraw-Hill.

Fluid Transport Equipment: Piping Systems

Pipe is used to transport fluids in process industries and daily life. This session includes materials about:

  1. Pressure Drop in Pipes and Pipe Fittings
  2. Pipe Wall Thickness, Pipe Size Selection, and Nominal Pipe Size (NPS)
  3. Control Valves Sizing
  4. Heuristics in Piping Design

Fluid Transport Equipment: Pumps and Compressors

Pump and compressor is used to provide energy to fluids, because of the energy loss by pipe friction. The materials discussed includes:

  1. Pump Theory
  2. Criteria for Pumps Selection
  3. Blowers, Fans, and Compressors
  4. Criteria for Compressors Selection
  5. Heuristics in Pump, and Compressor Design

Heat Exchangers: Single Phase Process

This session discuss about designing heat exchanger without invloving phase change, which also discuss:

  1. Basic Design Procedure and Theory
  2. Mean Temperature Difference
  3. Overall Heat Transfer Coefficient and Fouling Factors
  4. Types of Heat Exchangers
  5. TEMA Standards and Data Sheets
  6. Shell and Tube Exchangers Design without Phase Change
  7. Heuristics in Heat Exchangers Design
  8. Software: ASPEN Exchangers Design and Rating (EDR) or HTRI

Heat Exchangers: Process Involving Phase Change

This session will design heat exchanger, considering phase change in the process, and also discuss:

  1. Condensers
  2. Reboilers
  3. Evaporators
  4. Double Pipe Heat Exchangers
  5. Fired Heaters
  6. Heuristics in Heat Exchangers Design
  7. Solving the Problems with Computer Aid (Aspen Exchanger Design and Rating (EDR), HTRI, etc.)

Separation Towers: Distillation and Gas Absorption

This session discuss materials in:

  1. Vapor-Liquid Equilibria
  2. Single-stage Flash Calculations
  3. Binary Distillation (McCabe-Thiele Method)
  4. Batch Distillation
  5. Multicomponent Separation (Fenske-Underwood-Gilliland Method)
  6. Gas Absorption and Stripping Process
  7. Criteria for Selection of Separation Methods

Separation Towers: Mechanical Design

This session focuses on mechanical design of separation tower, which includes:

  1. Tray Towers Design
  2. Packed Towers Design
  3. Efficiencies of Trays and Packing
  4. Condenser and Reboiler Load
  5. Distillation Towers Sequencing
  6. Heuristics in Separation Towers Design
  7. Solving the Problems with Computer Aid (Aspen Hysys, Aspen Plus, etc.)

Separation Towers: Liquid-Liquid Extraction

This session discuss materials in:

  1. Ternary Diagram
  2. Solvent Selection
  3. Calculation of Stage Requirements
  4. Countercurrent Operation
  5. Equipment for Extraction

Pressure Vessel: Design Considerations

This session discuss materials in:

  1. Pressure Vessels Code and Standards
  2. Design Pressure and Temperature
  3. Materials Selection
  4. Maximum Allowable Stress and Welding-Joint Efficiency
  5. Corrosion Allowance
  6. Design of Vessels under Internal Pressure (Shell Thickness and Selection of Head and Closure)
  7. Design of Vessels under External Pressure

Pressure Vessels: Design Subject to Combined Loading

  4. Earthquake

Pressure Vessel: Process Vessels Design

  4. Batch Dryers

Mixing and Agitation

Mixing equipments use fluid mechanic concepts to design. So, the matrials discussed in this session covers:

  1. A Basic Stirred Tank Design (Geometry and Impellers)
  2. Vessel Flow Patterns
  3. Agitator Power Requirements
  4. Pipeline Mixers Design

Transfer of Solids

Solids are hard to transport. The materials discussed in this session will cover:

  1. Slurry Transport
  2. Pneumatic Conveying
  3. Mechanical Conveyors and Elevators
  4. Chutes
  5. Solids Feeders
